The state of West Bengal v/s Union of India

Power challenged : Extent of Parliament's power to legislate on matters that fall within state list CHALLENGED

Background

The case arose when the Union Government passed the Coal Bearing Areas (Acquisition and Development) Act, 1957, which allowed the central government to acquire land in any state for coal mining purposes.

The State of West Bengal challenged the constitutionality of this Act, arguing that the acquisition of land within a state fell under the State List (List II of the Seventh Schedule) and was therefore beyond the legislative competence of the Union Parliament.
